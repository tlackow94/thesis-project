Feasibility of Developing Highway-Adjacent Renewable Energy Resources

Problem Statement

California Senate Bill 200 has identified benchmarks for renewable energy development across the state. Despite ambitious targets, suitable land for solar development remains a challenge to identify. This project explores freeway medians and right-of-ways as an underutilized opportunity for expanding solar energy infrastructure in southern California.

Our Approach

This project utilizes Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) to evaluate freeway medians and right-of-ways across southern California. MCDA combines multiple parameters — including terrain characteristics and parcel ownership information — and assigns each a weighted value based on its importance to solar suitability. The result is a ranked map of candidate areas, from most to least suitable for solar development.

Project Goal

The goal is to produce a geographic database representing the sites, their location, and their ranking. A web map and dashboard will also be produced in order to visualize the results of the analysis.
